About TabTalks

At TabTalks, I focus on one simple idea: meetings should be worth people's time. Too often, sessions run long, drift off track, or end without clear decisions. Workshops feel busy but go nowhere. Conferences look good on paper but don't land. I help organizations fix that by designing gatherings that are structured, practical, and built to get real results.

I've spent more than ten years planning and leading meetings, workshops, and conferences for a wide range of organizations. No two groups are the same, and I don't treat them that way. A short leadership session, a hands-on working workshop, or a multi-day conference all need different pacing, tools, and energy. My role is to shape the experience so people stay involved, conversations stay focused, and the time invested actually pays off.

I start by getting clear on what you're trying to achieve and who's in the room. From there, I design the flow — the activities, the decision points, the moments where people need to think, talk, or build something together. I draw on tested facilitation approaches and practical design methods, then adapt them to your setting. The outcome is straightforward: stronger participation, clearer direction, and next steps people understand and can act on.

If you want your next meeting or event to feel purposeful — and maybe even a bit energizing — I'd be glad to help design it with you.
